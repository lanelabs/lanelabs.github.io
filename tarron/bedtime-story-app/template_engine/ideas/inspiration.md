# Template Inspiration

One-line rough ideas pulled from book blurbs, story structures,
opening techniques, and tension mechanics research. Each is a
candidate to develop into a full template. Not all will work —
the point is variety and sparking combinations.

Notation: {placeholders} show where story elements would go.
Lines marked * would need new placeholder forms or data.


## FROM BOOK BLURB PATTERNS

~~The "But" Pivot — ordinary setup shattered by one word~~ → **Used in Template 14**
> ~~{Character1} had a perfectly ordinary life in {setting}. But {hook}.~~

~~The Question Hook — open a curiosity gap~~ → **Used in Template 15**
> ~~What if {hook} wasn't a problem — but a gift?~~

~~The Catalog of Wonders — list of vivid details before narrative~~ → **Used in Template 16**
> ~~{Item}. {Setting.bare}. {Weather}. And in the middle of it all, {character1.emotional}.~~

~~The Unlikely Hero — smallness contrasted with bigness~~ → **Used in Template 17** (combined with Fool Triumphant)
> ~~{Character1.emotional} is the last person you'd expect to face {hook}. And yet.~~

~~The Anti-Blurb / Warning — reverse psychology~~ → **Used in Template 13** (combined with Contrast opening and Anti-Fairy-Tale feel)
> ~~This is not a gentle story. But then, neither is {character1.emotional}.~~

~~The Emotional Promise — feeling over plot~~ → **Used in Template 22** (combined with Kishotenketsu)
> ~~A {tone} story about two characters who discover that {moral.about} changes everything.~~

~~The Fairy-Tale Subversion — set up a trope, break it~~ → **Used in Template 18**
> ~~Once upon a time, {character1} rescued {character2}. Actually, it was the other way around.~~

~~The "What If" Premise — one impossible thing, stated plainly~~ → **Used in Template 19**
> ~~What if {hook} only happened in {setting}? And what if {character1.emotional} was the only one who noticed?~~

~~The Snowball — one thing leads to another leads to chaos~~ → **Used in Template 20** (combined with Story Spine)
> ~~It started small. Then {hook}. Then {item} showed up. Now {character1.emotional} and {character2} are in the middle of {storyShape}.~~

~~The Withholding Blurb — hint but don't explain~~ → **Used in Template 21**
> ~~Something is wrong in {setting}. {Character1.emotional} knows it. {Character2} knows it. Neither will say what.~~


## FROM STORY STRUCTURE FRAMEWORKS

~~Kishotenketsu (no conflict) — surprise recontextualization~~ → **Used in Template 22** (combined with Emotional Promise)
> ~~{Character1.emotional} and {character2} in {setting}, {weather}. Nothing goes wrong. But something shifts — and afterward, nothing looks quite the same.~~

~~Voyage and Return — wonder then homecoming~~ → **Used in Template 23**
> ~~{Character1.emotional} leaves {setting} and enters somewhere new. What starts as wonder becomes {hook}. {Item} is the only way back.~~

~~Rebirth — darkness to sudden light~~ → **Used in Template 24** (combined with Rags to Riches)
> ~~{Character1.emotional} has been trapped by {hook} for as long as they can remember. Then {character2} arrives in {setting} with {item}.~~

~~The Story Spine — "because of that" chain~~ → **Used in Template 20** (combined with Snowball)
> ~~Once upon a time, {character1.emotional} lived in {setting}. Every day, everything was the same. But one day, {hook}. Because of that, {item}. Because of that, {storyShape}.~~

~~Circular — ends where it began~~ → **Used in Template 25** (combined with Before/After)
> ~~It begins in {setting}. It ends there, too. But between the first step and {hook}, everything about {character1.emotional} and {character2} changes.~~

~~Wind-Down — energy narrows toward sleep~~ → **Used in Template 11** (combined with Direct Address)
> ~~{Weather}, {setting} grows quiet. {Character1.emotional} and {character2} settle into {opening}. Even {hook} feels far away now. {Item} rests nearby. Tomorrow, {storyShape}. Tonight, just {tone.noun}.~~

~~Man in Hole — things get worse, then better~~ → **Used in Template 26** (combined with Overcoming the Monster)
> ~~In {setting}, things were fine — until {hook}. {Character1.emotional} and {character2} fall deep. But {item} and {storyShape} pull them back up.~~

~~Overcoming the Monster — courage vs. overwhelming odds~~ → **Used in Template 26** (combined with Man in Hole)
> ~~Something terrible has come to {setting}: {hook}. Only {character1.emotional} and {character2} stand between it and everything they care about.~~

~~Rags to Riches — from nothing to everything~~ → **Used in Template 24** (combined with Rebirth)
> ~~{Character1.emotional} had nothing but {item}. In {setting}, {weather}, that turned out to be enough.~~

~~The Fool Triumphant — underestimated character wins~~ → **Used in Template 17** (combined with Unlikely Hero)
> ~~Nobody expected {character1.emotional} to solve {hook}. Not even {character2}. Especially not in {setting}.~~


## FROM OPENING TECHNIQUE PATTERNS

~~Legend Frame — "they say"~~ → **Used in Template 8** (combined with Believer vs. Skeptic)
> ~~They say that in {setting}, {weather}, {hook} happens once a generation. {Character1.emotional} and {character2} are about to find out if it's true.~~

~~Rule to Be Broken — prohibition stated~~ → **Used in Template 10**
> ~~The rule in {setting} was simple: never touch {item}. {Character1.emotional} wasn't good with rules.~~

~~"Everyone Knows" — common knowledge, quietly wrong~~ → **Used in Template 27**
> ~~Everyone in {setting} knew {hook} was impossible. {Character1.emotional} wasn't so sure.~~

~~Interrupted Routine — pattern broken~~ → **Used in Template 28** (combined with Mismatch/Cinderella combo)
> ~~Every day, {character1} did the same thing in {setting}. Until the day {character2.emotional} arrived with {item}.~~

~~Impossible Image — surreal and matter-of-fact~~ → **Used in Template 29** (combined with Mismatch/Dramatic Irony)
> ~~{Weather}, the {setting.bare} turned upside down. Not figuratively. {Character1.emotional} and {character2} noticed at the same time.~~

~~Dialogue Cold Open — voice before scene~~ → **Used in Template 12** (combined with Fragile Alliance)
> ~~"Don't touch that." {Character1.emotional} said it too late. {Character2} already had {item}.~~

~~Scalar Shift — zoom from vast to tiny (or reverse)~~ → **Used in Template 30** (also used in T24, T35)
> ~~Somewhere in the {setting.bare}, {weather}, smaller than anyone would notice, {item} began to glow. {Character1.emotional} noticed.~~

~~Sensory Opening — a sound or smell first~~ → **Used in Template 31** (combined with Seasonal/Cyclical and Sensory+Wind-Down+Lullaby combo)
> ~~The sound came first — {weather}. Then the smell of {setting.bare}. By the time {character1.emotional} saw {character2}, {hook} had already begun.~~

~~False Start — narrator corrects themselves~~ → **Used in Template 32**
> ~~This is a story about {character1}. Actually, no. This is a story about {character2.emotional} and how {item} changed everything.~~

~~Seasonal/Cyclical — anchored in natural time~~ → **Used in Template 31** (combined with Sensory Opening)
> ~~It was the kind of {weather} that only comes once a year. In {setting}, {character1.emotional} and {character2} both knew what it meant.~~

~~Direct Address — speaking to the listener~~ → **Used in Template 11** (combined with Wind-Down)
> ~~If you'd been standing in {setting} that night, {weather}, you'd have seen {character1.emotional} and {character2} staring at {item}. And you'd have wondered the same thing they did.~~

~~Origin Myth — creation scale~~ → **Used in Template 33** (combined with Origin Myth+Scarcity+Bookend combo)
> ~~Before anyone lived in {setting}, there was {item}. This is the story of how {character1.emotional} and {character2} found it — and what happened next.~~


## FROM TENSION MECHANICS

~~Reluctant Hero — the one who doesn't want to help~~ → **Used in Template 9**
> ~~{Character1.emotional} wants nothing to do with {hook}. But in {setting}, they're the only one who can help — and {character2} is counting on them.~~

~~Impossible Choice — two paths, both costly~~ → **Used in Template 34** (combined with Question Hook+Impossible Choice+Fairy-Tale combo)
> ~~In {setting}, {character1.emotional} faces {hook}. Help {character2}, or protect what they have. They can't do both.~~

~~Ticking Clock — deadline forces action~~ → **Used in Template 35**
> ~~{Weather}, something is changing in {setting}. {Character1.emotional} doesn't have long. {Hook} — and if they don't act, everything changes.~~

~~Secret About to Surface — dread of exposure~~ → **Used in Template 36** (combined with Emotional Wound)
> ~~{Character1.emotional} has been hiding something. In {setting}, {weather}, the truth is about to come out — and {character2} is the one who'll find it.~~

~~Fragile Alliance — need without trust~~ → **Used in Template 12** (combined with Dialogue Cold Open)
> ~~{Character1} and {character2.emotional} have every reason not to trust each other. But {hook} in {setting} means they have to try.~~

~~Scarcity — the last of something~~ → **Used in Template 37** (combined with The Promise)
> ~~It's just {item}. But in {setting}, {weather}, it's the last one — and both {character1.emotional} and {character2} know it.~~

~~Believer vs. Skeptic — worldview collision~~ → **Used in Template 8** (combined with Legend Frame)
> ~~{Character1.emotional} is sure {hook} is real. {Character2} thinks it's nonsense. {Setting}, {weather}, is about to settle the argument.~~

~~Before/After — the world changed~~ → **Used in Template 25** (combined with Circular)
> ~~{Setting} used to be safe. Before {hook}. Now {character1.emotional} and {character2} are the only ones left — and they have {item}.~~

~~Mismatch / Dramatic Irony — character contradicts situation~~ → **Used in Template 29** (combined with Impossible Image)
> ~~{Character1.emotional} — facing {hook}. In {setting}. {Weather}. It would be funny if it weren't so serious.~~

~~The Promise — a vow at stake~~ → **Used in Template 37** (combined with Scarcity)
> ~~{Character1.emotional} made a promise: {moral}. Keeping it in {setting}, with {hook} bearing down, might cost everything.~~

~~The Emotional Wound — past pain makes this harder~~ → **Used in Template 36** (combined with Secret About to Surface)
> ~~For {character1.emotional}, {hook} isn't just a problem — it's the same thing that went wrong last time. And this time, {character2} is watching.~~

~~The "Must" Verb — obligation and reluctance compressed~~ → **Absorbed into Template 9**
> ~~{Character1.emotional} must face {hook} in {setting}. Not because they want to — because {character2} can't.~~

~~Voice as Hook — personality of the telling~~ → **Used in Template 38**
> ~~Listen — {character1.emotional} didn't plan any of this. Nobody plans for {hook}. But here they are, in {setting}, {weather}, with nothing but {item} and {character2}.~~

~~Interrupted Routine + Mismatch + Cinderella Arc~~ → **Used in Template 28** (combined with Interrupted Routine)

~~Catalog + Reluctant Hero + Three-Beat~~ → **Used in Template 16** (combined with Catalog of Wonders)
> ~~{Item}. {Setting.bare}. {Hook}. {Character1.emotional} wanted no part of it. {Character2} needed all of it. {Weather}, something had to give.~~

~~Sensory + Wind-Down + Lullaby Feel~~ → **Used in Template 31** (combined with Sensory Opening and Seasonal/Cyclical)

~~Origin Myth + Scarcity + Bookend~~ → **Used in Template 33** (combined with Origin Myth)
> ~~Before there was {setting}, there was {item}. {Character1.emotional} and {character2} are the only ones who remember. {Hook} threatens to erase even that. But {item} endures.~~
