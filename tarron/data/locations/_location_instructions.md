# Location Descriptions

These are generic fantasy locations for use across apps and auto-generators.

## Voice and Style

- **Sensory atmosphere first**: lead with what you see, hear, smell, or feel the moment you arrive
- **Imply danger or opportunity**: suggest what might happen here, what waits, or what draws travelers
- Keep descriptions **loose on specifics** so a storyteller can adapt the location to any setting
- Vary sentence structure. Use periods and commas to break up ideas rather than relying on dashes
- Vivid and evocative but **concise**, one to two sentences
- Target length: **30 words or fewer**
- **No special characters**: no em dashes, curly quotes, or unicode symbols. Use plain ASCII only

## What to Include

- The **atmosphere** of the place: what it feels like to stand there
- Just enough **sensory detail** to ground the location (the smell of sulfur, silk catching light, water lapping at stone)
- The **storytelling direction** of what might happen here (discovery, ambush, refuge, mystery)
- **History or consequence** when it adds character (who built it, why it was abandoned, what went wrong)

## What to Exclude

- Mechanical details: specific measurements, distances, population counts, or stat-like information
- Exhaustive inventories: don't list every feature, room, or occupant
- Game-system language: no AP, HP, challenge ratings, encounter levels
- Meta instructions: nothing about tables, guides, narrators, or players

## Examples

Good, sensory atmosphere with implied danger:
> The mountain breathes. Ash drifts like snow, the ground trembles underfoot, and the air tastes of sulfur and the end of things.

Good, atmosphere with implied opportunity:
> Cool water in the killing heat. Palm shade and birdsong where nothing should survive. Not every gift in the desert is what it seems.

Good, mood with history:
> The walls still stand but the gates hang open. Something drove the garrison out in a hurry. Their belongings remain, untouched.

Good, concise and sensory:
> Silk threads catch the light between the trees. The webs grow thicker the deeper you go. Something large is patient here.

Good, urban atmosphere:
> The air hums with residual enchantment. Signs glow without flame. Cats watch from windowsills with too-knowing eyes, and the alleys rearrange themselves after dark.

Bad, too mechanical:
> A 30-foot tall stone fortification with 4 guard towers, each manned by 2-3 guards. Contains a barracks for 50 soldiers and a mess hall.
