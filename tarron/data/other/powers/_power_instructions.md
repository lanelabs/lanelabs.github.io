# Power Descriptions

These are generic fantasy powers for use across apps and auto-generators.

## Voice and Style

- **Cryptic narrative hook** followed by **grounding imagery**: lead with a lore-like statement that evokes mood or consequence, then anchor it with just enough concrete description to convey what the power actually does
- **Hint at danger or caution** when it fits the power naturally: consequences of failure, a cost to the wielder, or a warning. Not every power needs this, but lean into it when the power invites it
- Keep descriptions **loose on specifics** so a storyteller can adapt the idea to fit their scene, but **clear enough** that someone unfamiliar with the concept understands what it is
- Vary sentence structure. Use periods and commas to break up ideas rather than relying on dashes
- Vivid and evocative but **concise**, one to two sentences
- Target length: **30 words or fewer**. Cut anything beyond that
- **No special characters**: no em dashes, curly quotes, or unicode symbols. Use plain ASCII only

## What to Include

- The **narrative feel** of what it means to wield this power or to face it
- Just enough **concrete imagery** to ground the power (a roaring sphere of flame, cold pulseless skin, a tiny bouncing ball)
- The **storytelling direction** of what it enables in a scene (escape, revelation, devastation, deception)
- **Consequences, costs, or irony** when they're inherent to the power

## What to Exclude

- Mechanical details: specific damage values, durations, ranges, hit points, costs
- Tiered breakdowns: no "at basic level... at greater power... at its peak..." ladders
- Exhaustive rules: don't list every option, condition, or edge case
- Game-system language: no AP, HP, rolls, turns, rounds, sessions, scenes
- Meta instructions: nothing about tables, guides, narrators, or players

## Examples

Good, cryptic hook with grounding imagery:
> A roaring sphere of flame hurled across the distance. They say a wizard's first fireball changes them. Not the power, but the silence after.

Good, narrative feel with hint of danger:
> Absolute dominion over another's will. Obedience without hesitation, without question. But the magic remembers what you made them do, and so will they.

Good, lore-like with just enough description:
> A tiny spectral companion with its own opinions and a loyalty that must be earned. Mistreat it, and one morning you'll wake up alone.

Good, concise, playful, grounded:
> A tiny bouncing ball that avoids the living and destroys everything else: pottery, glass, furniture, dignity. Harmless to creatures. Devastating to rooms.

Bad, too mechanical, too long:
> Summoning a spectral warhammer into the grip. At its most powerful, the hammer strikes with enough force to scatter lesser foes and deal significant damage to powerful enemies. The hammer persists for up to a minute and can be thrown, returning to the caster's hand. At lesser power levels, it deals moderate damage to a single target.
